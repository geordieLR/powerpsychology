Frequently asked questions

Useful information before you enquire.

Availability, funding and clinical suitability can vary. These answers explain the general process.

Is Power Psychology accepting new clients?

Availability can differ between private clients, funded or ACC-related referrals and professional referrals. Make a brief enquiry and the practice can confirm current capacity and the appropriate next step.

Does Power Psychology accept ACC referrals?

Power Psychology has experience working across ACC contracts. The applicable contract, eligibility, referral route, clinical fit and current availability must be confirmed for each referral.

Do I need to share my full history in the enquiry form?

No. Please share only enough information to identify the referral type and the broad reason for seeking support. Detailed clinical information should be discussed through an appropriate secure process.

Who does Power Psychology work with?

The practice works with adults aged 18 years and over. Visible areas of practice include trauma and PTSD, anxiety, EMDR, maternal mental health, neuropsychological assessment and professional supervision.

Are online sessions available?

Telehealth is available where it is clinically appropriate and the client has a private space, suitable device and stable internet connection.

Is the office physically accessible?

The office is on the second floor and does not have a lift. Telehealth may be an alternative where suitable. Please mention accessibility needs when enquiring.

What are the current fees?

The existing published fees are $239 for individual therapy, $239 for the listed neuropsychological-assessment session and $230 for professional supervision. Confirm current fees and funding arrangements before booking.

What is the cancellation policy?

The current published policy requests 48 hours’ notice. It states that cancellations within 24–48 hours may incur an $80 fee and cancellations within 24 hours may incur the full fee. Confirm the current terms when arranging an appointment.

Is this an urgent or crisis service?

No. If someone is in immediate danger call 111. For urgent mental-health support, contact the appropriate local crisis team, health provider or emergency service.
